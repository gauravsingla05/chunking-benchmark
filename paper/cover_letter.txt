Dear Editor-in-Chief,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript titled "Position-Aware vs Semantic Chunking for Content Generation: Small Gains, Big Trade-offs" for consideration as an Original Research Article in IEEE Access.

This study addresses a practical gap in Large Language Model (LLM) literature: while document chunking is a ubiquitous preprocessing step in RAG and content generation, there is limited empirical guidance on which strategies actually improve downstream quality. This work provides this guidance through a controlled evaluation of four chunking methods across 101 research papers.

Key Contributions:

Empirical Benchmarking: I demonstrate that simple truncation outperforms or matches sophisticated chunking methods (semantic breakpoint, position-aware) for document-to-slide generation tasks.

Statistical Rigor: I provide statistical evidence (paired t-tests, effect sizes) quantifying the marginal differences between approaches.

Universal Limitations: I identify statistics retention as a universal limitation across all methods, suggesting this is a generation-side rather than chunking-side challenge.

The findings have immediate practical implications: practitioners can confidently utilize simple truncation instead of complex chunking pipelines, reducing system complexity without sacrificing quality.
